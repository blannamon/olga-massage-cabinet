The website is a conversion-focused bilingual landing page for Olga’s massage practice in Tiraspol, with services, personal credentials, reviews, messaging contacts, and booking as the central user journey. 

## Layout Structure

The page uses a conventional centered desktop layout with several full-width background bands.

**Desktop grid**

* Approximate maximum content width: **1180–1240 px**
* Likely grid: **12 columns**
* Outer page gutters: approximately **48–64 px**
* Internal grid gaps: approximately **20–28 px**
* Most content aligns to the same left and right container boundaries, creating a consistent vertical axis.

The page alternates between:

* Full-width sections with contained inner content
* Two-column editorial layouts
* Three- or four-column card grids
* Narrow centered section headings

The hero is visually full-width but internally divided into two zones:

* Left: approximately **43–47%** for the value proposition
* Right: approximately **53–57%** for the massage image

The content hierarchy becomes progressively more informational:

1. Immediate offer
2. Trust indicators
3. Services
4. Practitioner credibility
5. Social proof
6. Objection handling
7. Contact and booking

### Responsiveness strategy

The screenshot only shows the desktop composition, so the mobile behavior must be inferred.

Recommended breakpoints:

* **Large desktop:** 1200 px and above
* **Tablet:** 768–1199 px
* **Mobile:** below 768 px

On tablet, three-column sections should become two columns. On mobile:

* The hero should stack vertically
* Text should appear before the hero image
* Services and testimonials should become single-column cards
* The four benefits should become a two-column or horizontally scrollable list
* FAQ columns should merge into one accordion
* Contact details and form should stack
* The header should collapse into a hamburger menu
* The main booking CTA should remain highly visible, potentially through a sticky bottom action bar

A four-column mobile grid with approximately **20 px side padding** would be appropriate.

## Section Order

### 1. Header

Contains the Olga logo, primary navigation, and the main “Записаться” booking button.

Its purpose is to establish the brand and provide direct access to the major page sections without creating a complex navigation system.

### 2. Hero

The hero presents:

* Main headline: professional massage in Tiraspol
* Short outcome-oriented description
* Primary booking CTA
* Secondary messaging CTA
* Compact trust statement
* Large lifestyle photograph of the massage process

Its purpose is to answer four questions immediately:

* What service is offered?
* Where is it offered?
* What benefit does the customer receive?
* How can the customer book?

### 3. Benefits / Trust Strip

A horizontal row of four compact advantages:

* Individual approach
* Comfortable treatment room
* Support for local and international clients
* Convenient booking

This section reduces uncertainty before the visitor reaches the service list. It functions as a lightweight trust and differentiation layer rather than a conventional feature-card section.

### 4. Services

A centered “Услуги” heading followed by three service cards.

Each card contains:

* Service image
* Service name
* Short explanation
* Session duration
* Starting price

A secondary button below the cards allows the visitor to see the complete service list.

The purpose is to make the offer concrete and allow visitors to quickly identify an appropriate treatment.

### 5. About Olga

A two-column editorial section:

* Portrait photograph on the left
* Biography and credentials on the right

The text is supported by three compact credential indicators:

* Professional education
* More than five years of experience in the concept
* Quality oils and materials

This section humanizes the service and addresses the trust barrier associated with health, physical contact, and personal care.

### 6. Testimonials

A centered heading followed by three testimonial cards arranged as a carousel.

Each card includes:

* Quotation mark
* Review text
* Client avatar
* Client name and location
* Star rating

Previous and next controls sit outside the card row.

The section provides social proof and also reinforces the positioning for both local and foreign clients.

### 7. FAQ

A centered heading followed by two columns of accordion questions.

The questions cover:

* How to book
* Whether early arrival is required
* Contraindications
* What to bring
* Card payment
* Support for international clients

This section handles common objections without requiring the visitor to leave the page or contact Olga first.

### 8. Contact and Lead Form

This section is split into two unequal columns.

The left side contains:

* Decorative interior photograph
* Phone number
* Telegram
* Viber
* WhatsApp
* Instagram

The right side contains a compact enquiry form with:

* Name
* Phone number
* Preferred communication method
* Message
* Submit button

Its purpose is to convert visitors who have not used one of the earlier booking buttons.

### 9. Footer

The footer contains:

* Repeated Olga logo
* Short positioning statement
* Phone number
* Address
* Social-media icons
* Copyright
* Privacy-policy link

It closes the page with a darker olive brand block and repeats the essential contact information.

## Navigation

The desktop header follows a three-zone structure:

* **Left:** logo and wordmark
* **Center:** navigation links
* **Right:** primary booking CTA

The visible menu items are:

* Услуги
* Обо мне
* Отзывы
* FAQ
* Контакты

These should function as anchor links to sections on the same page. Smooth scrolling is appropriate, provided it respects the user’s reduced-motion preferences.

There is no need for dropdown menus because the information architecture is shallow.

### Sticky behavior

Sticky behavior is not visibly confirmed by the static reference, but it would be appropriate for implementation.

Recommended behavior:

* Header becomes sticky after leaving the top of the page
* Background changes from solid warm white to slightly translucent cream
* Add a subtle bottom border or light shadow
* Reduce the header height slightly while scrolling
* Preserve the booking button

### Mobile navigation

On mobile:

* Logo remains on the left
* Hamburger control appears on the right
* Navigation opens as a full-width drawer or overlay
* Menu links should be large, vertically spaced touch targets
* A full-width booking button should appear at the bottom of the menu

The language selector for Russian and English should also be clearly visible in the mobile menu or header.

* Header horizontal padding: **48–64 px**
* Header vertical padding: **16–22 px**
* Hero content padding: **64–88 px**
* Standard section vertical padding: **64–88 px**
* Major section separation: **72–104 px**
* Card gaps: **20–24 px**

## Image Treatment

The image direction is warm, intimate, and editorial.

### Hero image

The hero uses a large landscape photograph with:

* Soft natural or diffused studio light
* Warm beige interior
* Neutral clothing and textiles
* Green plant accents
* Candles and amber bottles
* Shallow or moderate depth of field

The image occupies the full height of the hero’s right side and uses `object-fit: cover`.

There is no strong dark overlay. The subject remains clear and the photograph integrates with the cream text area through shared warm tones.

### Service images

Service cards use wide landscape crops, approximately **16:9** or slightly taller.

The photography focuses on:

* Hands
* Massage techniques
* Oils
* Back, shoulder, and neck treatments

Faces are mostly excluded, allowing the visitor to focus on the treatment rather than a specific client.

### About portrait

The portrait is closer to a **4:3** ratio and has a warm lifestyle-studio appearance.

It visually matches the massage environment through:

* Candlelight
* Beige clothing
* Soft brown background
* Plant details
* Warm skin tones

### Contact image

The contact section uses a decorative still life rather than another practitioner image:

* Vase
* Green branch
* Candle
* Warm wall

This prevents visual repetition and creates a calm transition into the utility-focused contact area.

### Image processing

All images should share:

* Warm white balance
* Slightly reduced saturation
* Soft contrast
* Natural skin tones
* Beige and olive environmental elements
* No aggressive filters
* No obvious stock-photo appearance

A light cream color grade across all images would make the site feel like one coherent photo session.

## Cards and Content Blocks

### Service cards

The service cards use:

* White or warm-white background
* Thin neutral border
* Small corner radius, approximately **6–10 px**
* Little or no visible shadow
* Full-width image at the top
* Left-aligned content
* Metadata aligned along the bottom edge

Internal padding is approximately **16–20 px**.

The duration and price are visually separated:

* Duration on the left with a clock icon
* Price on the right
* Price receives slightly stronger weight or larger type

Cards should use equal heights so that prices align across the row.

### Benefit blocks

The four benefits are not heavy cards. They consist of:

* Circular outlined icon
* Short bold label
* One or two lines of supporting text

Vertical separators may be used between items on desktop.

This keeps the block visually light and prevents unnecessary container nesting.

### Credential blocks

The about-section credentials use line icons and compact text labels. They are arranged horizontally and do not require visible card backgrounds.

### Testimonial cards

Testimonials use:

* Warm white background
* Thin border
* Small radius
* Generous internal padding
* Large decorative quotation mark
* Client identity anchored near the bottom

The avatars are circular and intentionally small so the review text remains dominant.

### FAQ blocks

The FAQ uses border-separated accordion rows rather than independent floating cards.

Each row contains:

* Question aligned left
* Chevron icon aligned right
* Expandable answer below

The open state should rotate the chevron and reveal the answer with a subtle height and opacity transition.

### Form block

Fields use:

* Light neutral background or white

The textarea is larger but follows the same visual system.

## Overall Design Feel

The design is best described as:

* Premium but accessible
* Calm and wellness-oriented
* Editorial
* Natural
* Personal rather than corporate
* Conversion-focused
* Suitable for a local independent specialist

The serif typography, botanical logo, warm photography, olive accents, and cream surfaces create an atmosphere of safety, competence, and personal care.

It avoids the visual clichés of medical clinics: bright blue, sterile white, rigid grids, and technical imagery. At the same time, it does not become overly decorative or spa-like. The result sits between a private healthcare practice and a boutique wellness studio.

The central experience is deliberately reassuring. The page repeatedly communicates:

* Olga is a real, identifiable practitioner
* The environment is clean and comfortable
* The treatment is individualized
* Booking is simple
* Foreign clients can communicate comfortably
* The visitor can contact Olga through familiar messaging apps

For a faithful reconstruction, the most important principles are consistent warm photography, restrained olive accents, serif-led hierarchy, thin borders, compact cards, and a clear booking action throughout the page.
